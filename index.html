Land in Geneva and drive over the Alps — a Chamonix lunch under Mont Blanc, a night in the Barolo hills — then down into Tuscany for three nights in a Chianti wine hamlet, three in Florence, and three on the Maremma coast, before flying home from Rome. L'Arte di Vivere
The Art of Living

The Authenticity Playbook

  1. Eat OUT, not in

    One world-class meal per leg. Every other night: a village osteria, a harbor seafood place, the trattoria on your block.

  2. Visit towns at living hours

    7–9am and 6–9pm. Midday is emptiest of locals, fullest of day-trippers. The passeggiata is everything.

  3. Anchor each visit to a transaction

    Buy the pecorino, the wine, the lunch. A purchase puts you in the life. A photo doesn't.

  4. Favor small towns

    Castelnuovo Berardenga at dinner beats Siena's Campo at noon. Famous ones early, small ones in the evening.

  5. The wine is the most authentic luxury

    A cellar tasting with the people who grew the Sangiovese is the real thing, not a staged experience.

  6. In Florence, live rather than visit

    Coffee at the corner bar, dinner on your block. Cross the river to the monuments by day, sleep in the neighborhood.

Buon a Sapersi
Good to Know

Practical Notes

  • Why driving: Simplon rail line (Geneva–Milan) closed June 26 – July 26, 2026. Driving sidesteps it and turns arrival into an Alps road trip.
  • Two rentals: Car 1 Geneva→Florence (cross-border, Sixt), Car 2 Florence→FCO (domestic, Avis). Split avoids Florence garage fees.
  • Alpine costs: Mont Blanc tunnel (~€53), Swiss vignette (~CHF 40), Italian tolls, fuel.
  • ZTL zones: Never drive into Florence or Siena historic centers — camera fines. Park outside the walls.
  • July rhythm: Mornings and evenings out, siesta 1–4 PM. Book earliest museum slots.
  • Boat day: Skippered only. Specify calm shallow bays and life jackets.
  • Saturnia: Water shoes, towels, change of clothes in the car.
