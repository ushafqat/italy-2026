Land in Geneva and drive over the Alps — a Chamonix lunch under Mont Blanc, a night in the Barolo hills — then down into Tuscany for three nights in a Chianti wine hamlet, three in Florence, and three on the Maremma coast, before flying home from Rome. The arc runs Alpine drive → wine country → city → coast.

Day by Day

The Itinerary

Day 1 · Sat Jul 4 Geneva → Chamonix → Langhe

10:45 AM — Land in Geneva, pick up the Sixt (Mercedes C-Class). On the road.

Noon — Chamonix (~1h) — light lunch on a terrace under Mont Blanc. No hike; you're jet-lagged.

Afternoon — Mont Blanc tunnel → Aosta → past Turin into the Langhe (~3h). Arrive Arborina Relais, La Morra ~5 PM. Sunset over the Barolo vines.

Dinner — Arborina's restaurant, or Bovio in La Morra village, or Alba (~15 min).

Day 2 · Sun Jul 5 Langhe → Seaside → Chianti

9:30 AM — Breakfast at Arborina, then drive the coastal route (A26→A12).

12:15 PM — Forte dei Marmi, Versilia coast — long seaside lunch at a beach club.

~5 PM — Arrive Borgo San Felice. Cellar tasting of the estate's own labels (Vigorello, the first Super Tuscan).

Dinner — On-property, or a Castelnuovo Berardenga trattoria.

Day 3 · Mon Jul 6 Siena + Chianti

Early — Siena (20 min) — Piazza del Campo and Duomo before the buses. Park at Santa Caterina escalator lots. Never enter the ZTL.

Midday — Lunch where the menu's handwritten. Buy something from a shop.

Afternoon — Pool / spa during peak heat.

Evening — Small Chianti village at golden hour — passeggiata, aperitivo, dinner where it's mostly Italians.

Day 4 · Tue Jul 7 Chianti Wine Route or Montalcino
Option A — Chianti Classico Loop

Radda, Gaiole, Castellina. Cellar visits, slow lunch, SR222 backroads.

Option B — Montalcino (~1h)

Brunello tastings at the fortezza, Sant'Antimo abbey, long lunch.

Evening — World-class meal on purpose — Osteria di Passignano (Antinori, Michelin, ~40 min) or a marquee winery lunch.

Day 5 · Wed Jul 8 Drive to Florence → Oltrarno

Morning — San Felice → Florence (~1h15). Return Car 1 at Florence Central Train Station. Tram T2 or taxi into center.

Afternoon — Check into Oltrarno Airbnb, Ponte Vecchio side. Walk to the bridge, Pitti, the Duomo.

Evening — First gelato, the Duomo lit at night, dinner in the Oltrarno.

Day 6 · Thu Jul 9 Florence — Renaissance Day

8:15 AM — Uffizi at opening (timed entry).

Morning — Duomo complex + Baptistery doors.

Lunch — Mercato di Sant'Ambrogio, with the locals.

Afternoon — Siesta (1–4 PM July rhythm).

Evening — Aperitivo in Piazza Santo Spirito, dinner in San Frediano.

Day 7 · Fri Jul 10 Florence — Slow Oltrarno

Morning — Accademia early for David (timed entry).

Midday — Coffee at the corner bar, artisan workshops on Via Maggio, Boboli, long lunch, siesta.

Sunset — Piazzale Michelangelo.

Evening — The one big Florence dinner — book in advance.

Day 8 · Sat Jul 11 Drive to the Coast → L'Andana

Morning — Tram/taxi to FLR, pick up Car 2 (Avis, Mercedes C-Class). Florence → L'Andana (~2h). Optional Massa Marittima stop.

Afternoon — Arrive, unpack, estate mode. Sunset on the terrace.

Note — Tell concierge: beach-club tomorrow, ask about skippered boat.

Day 9 · Sun Jul 12 Estate + Beach Club

Morning — Slow breakfast. Couples massages (included).

Afternoon — Shuttle to La Cannucce beach club — loungers reserved, lunch at the club.

Evening — Adults-only pool, or Castiglione della Pescaia — fortress at sunset, seafood on the canal harbor.

Day 10 · Mon Jul 13 Flex Day
A — Boat

Skippered half/full day from Castiglione, Punta Ala, or Talamone. Calm shallow bays, life jackets. Morning = calmer. Sunset cruise = lower-key.

B — Tufa Towns

Pitigliano (~1h15, cliff-carved town) + Saturnia thermal cascades. Water shoes + towels.

C — Nothing

Pool, spa, a book under a pine tree. Castiglione harbor dinner to close.

Day 11 · Tue Jul 14 Fly Home — FCO → NYC

~8:30 AM — Out the door. ~2h via Via Aurelia to Rome Fiumicino. Drop Car 2 at FCO.

11:05 AM — AA 235 departs FCO → NYC. Arrives 2:35 PM.

Keeping It Real

The Authenticity Playbook

  1. Eat OUT, not in

    One world-class meal per leg. Every other night: a village osteria, a harbor seafood place, the trattoria on your block.

  2. Visit towns at living hours

    7–9am and 6–9pm. Midday is emptiest of locals, fullest of day-trippers.

  3. Anchor each visit to a transaction

    Buy the pecorino, the wine, the lunch. A purchase puts you in the life. A photo doesn't.

  4. Favor small towns

    Castelnuovo Berardenga at dinner beats Siena's Campo at noon. Famous ones early, small ones in the evening.

  5. The wine is the most authentic luxury

    A cellar tasting with the people who grew the Sangiovese is the real thing.

  6. In Florence, live rather than visit

    Coffee at the corner bar, dinner on your block. Cross the river to the monuments by day, sleep in the neighborhood.

Good to Know

Practical Notes

  • Why driving: Simplon rail line (Geneva–Milan) closed June 26 – July 26, 2026. Driving sidesteps it and turns arrival into an Alps road trip.
  • Two rentals: Car 1 Geneva→Florence (cross-border, Sixt), Car 2 Florence→FCO (domestic, Avis). Split avoids Florence garage fees.
  • Alpine costs: Mont Blanc tunnel (~€53), Swiss vignette (~CHF 40), Italian tolls, fuel.
  • ZTL zones: Never drive into Florence or Siena historic centers — camera fines. Park outside the walls.
  • July rhythm: Mornings and evenings out, siesta 1–4 PM. Book earliest museum slots.
  • Boat day: Skippered only. Specify calm shallow bays and life jackets.
  • Saturnia: Water shoes, towels, change of clothes in the car.
